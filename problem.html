Problem Statement

When travelers plan their own trips, all the time, effort, and stress—compounded further by the constraints of group travel—can sour the experience. While the time and effort can be cut down with travel agents and tour guides, they lack the personalized touch of planning for yourself and your needs.

76% of travelers turn to planning applications and websites to make the experience easier, but they only provide generic itineraries that lack personalization and flexibility. These leave out the hidden gems that make a trip memorable and adapt poorly to unforeseen complications.

Travelers are left choosing between an expensive or time-consuming planning stage, or settling for a trip that simply isn’t the experience they were hoping for.

Key Challenges

  • Choice Overload: Too many options can lead to decision fatigue.
  • Generic Itineraries: Lack of personalization results in missed local experiences.
  • Inflexible Plans: Rigid itineraries do not accommodate spontaneous changes.
  • Group Coordination: Difficulty aligning the preferences of multiple travelers.
